The King of BlackJack

A unique blend of Blackjack and roguelike elements. Players build a dynamic deck of cards by betting them in Blackjack, which is then used in combat. Cards feature custom, systemic effects based on rarity, triggering effects on hit (e.g., fire, execute, slow) or as passive effects while held in left hand (e.g., bonus damage, AOE damage nearby player). The goal is to defeat rounds of enemies and progress to higher rounds.

This project was a 3 person team effort developed at Masaryk university as part of Game development course.
I focused on engineering the backend, creating the flexible Card Effect System utilizing the Mediator pattern to manage countless card combinations without dependency issues. I also authored the core Player Controller, ensuring polished movement and reliable interaction via a custom raycast-based ground check.

C#, Unity URP, Teamwork

FlowerPhobia

In the game you take role of a child who is afraid of flowers and need to destroy a grandmas garden while escaping her grasp and dodging stones. Destroy continously spawning flowers to lower your anxiety, use gnomes as grenades against flowers and aim to reach high score.

A first-person game prototype delivered by a three-person team within a strict 72-hour game jam.
I was responsible for implementing key gameplay mechanics and systems, including the throwing gnome mechanic (used for AOE flower killing and associated pickup logic), the stone stun effect on the player, and the flower destruction system. This project delivered crucial experience in fast coding under pressure, collaborating effectively via Git to meet the tight 72-hour deadline.

Earth Transport

Developed a system for generating terrain features (countries, cities, forestry) by processing and serializing real-world open data using a third-party Delaunay triangulation library. I also implemented a system for reading serialized weather data to dynamically enhance visual realism. The project features a custom camera controller that rotates around the planet, built to support simple transport tycoon-style gameplay on top of the generated world.

This is the oldest project and I would like to make newer version using the experience I gained using compute shaders during my thesis to create more detailed world with faster generation speed and expand on the gameplay.
